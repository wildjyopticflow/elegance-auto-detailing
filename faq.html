Myths & Misconceptions

Paint Protection Film — The Real Story

PPF is a great product — but there's a lot of inaccurate marketing out there. Here's the honest breakdown.

Are PPF and Vinyl the same thing? +
No — other than both being films applied to a vehicle, they are completely different products.

Vinyl is a thin layer (roughly 2 mils) of cast or calendered PVC with pigments added for color. It's very malleable and highly conformable, allowing it to wrap over complex shapes and edges.

PPF is a much thicker (8–12 mils) multi-layered polyurethane film. It's designed to be perfectly clear and have impact and abrasion resistance. Most premium PPF films today also have self-healing properties. Being thicker and more resistant also means it has some limitations in terms of conformability.
Will a PPF install be 100% perfect / invisible? +
The short answer is no. PPF installation is not easy — the material is expensive and it takes many years to become a proficient installer. Even an experienced installer will have some "blemishes." Here's what to expect:

Water bubbles/air pockets will dry out after a few weeks once the film cures. If they don't completely evaporate, a hypodermic needle can release the air.

Lift lines occur during installation when part of the film is temporarily set while another area is worked on. These generally level down over time and are simply part of the process.

Dust specs may occasionally be present. Most installers work in a clean shop but not a surgically sealed clean room.

Remember: it's Paint PROTECTION Film, not Paint PERFECTION Film.
Will I see seams in my installation? +
On almost every panel — no. But certain parts of a vehicle have too complex a shape for film to fully contour to in a single piece. Bumpers, mirrors, and trunks are often done in multiple pieces, meaning there will be seams.

A skilled installer places seams in areas where they get lost naturally — curve reflections, edges with high reflection, or hidden areas. When a visible seam is unavoidable, we minimize it as much as possible and align both edges as closely as possible.
How long does PPF last? +
Past the 5-year mark, warranty length is mostly marketing. PPF is a sacrificial film — it absorbs damage so your paint doesn't have to. After 5 years of taking abuse, some panels will likely need replacement.

We always advise clients to focus on the quality of the film and installer rather than the length of the warranty. Durability varies based on: which panels are covered (a bumper takes far more abuse than a quarter panel), how well the car is maintained, how much time it spends on the road vs. in a garage, and whether there's been any physical damage.

Most people don't keep their cars more than 4–5 years anyway — a quality install that lasts that long is a complete success.
Are all PPF installations the same? +
Absolutely not. Two restaurants can serve a steak — but not all chefs cook at the same level.

Even if two shops use the same film brand, the quality of the installation itself can vary dramatically. Doing your research and choosing your installer carefully is more important than focusing on brand, warranty length, or price.

If you're looking for a clean, custom install with virtually no visible seams, you'll likely pay a bit more than at a pre-cut, done-in-a-day shop. That's a choice to make based on your priorities — but we believe the difference is always visible.
